**Labels**: `question`, `development`, `integration`
**Status**: 🧭 **Decision drafted** — **annotated (visually-rich) Markdown** as the single LLM input
format, PAGE/ALTO/TEITOK as the spatial source of truth, HTML/DOCX/PDF as inputs/intermediates only.
Pending empirical checks: **Docling/Marker PoC** on the hardest PDFs and the **#22 format bake-off**.

## 🎯 Goal
From the issue's format × detail matrix (PDF · InDesign XML · HTML+CSS · DOCX · PAGE/ALTO XML · MD ·
TXT), decide the format(s) that feed the LLMs — grounded in what existing DU benchmarks actually
use — toward a FAIR standard collection.

## 🧭 The answer in one picture
- **Annotated MD is the diet.** Vanilla MD's gaps vs PAGE/ALTO (page borders, positions, fonts) are
  restored by HTML-comment cues at near-zero token cost — exactly what #10's
  `api_util/doc_to_visual_md.py` + `layout_md.py` now emit (`PAGE_BREAK`, `BBOX`, `FONT`, `STYLE`,
  headers/footers, GFM tables), a superset of the DR's bare `<!-- Page N -->` marker.
- **Benchmarks corroborate the two-track design** (plan §2): end-to-end parsing is scored **in MD**
  (OmniDocBench); recognition ground truth is **PAGE/ALTO**; tables score as **HTML-via-TEDS**
  (already in `eval_metrics.py`); KIE uses JSON. No benchmark feeds raw HTML+CSS or IDML to a text
  LLM — so HTML stays an intermediate, IDML is skipped, TXT is fallback.
- **Grobid**: scholarly-PDF specialist emitting TEI — wrong fit for scan-heavy archaeological
  reports as the general route; keep as a bake-off candidate for the digital-born scholarly slice
  only (plan §4).
- **Page-classification routing** decides *treatment* per page (extract vs OCR vs table-parser), not
  output format — output stays annotated MD (plan §4).
- **FAIR roadmap layer** (`SKILL.md`/`INDEX.md` agentic navigation) is the DR's end-state —
  **deferred** until #22 proves the basic MD pipeline on in-domain scans (plan §3.5).
